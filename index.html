Hi. We are Paniki.

And this is our project, Analyzing the Relationship Between Environmental Factors and Bat Population in the Philippines, This project aims to analyze the correlation of different environmental factors such as climate, humidity, wildlife, etc. to the population of bats in the Philippines. This project also aims to highlight the bats species that are most endangered due to changing climate.

Here's an overview of our project.

Motivation

According to Tanalgo and Hughes, there are many species of bats in the Philippines and are very important to the Philippines' ecosystem and biodiversity. Many of these species are only found in the Philippines and some may be endangered due to climate change.

Problem

There isn't much research on the effect of environmental factors on bat population in the Philippines. Because of this, it is hard to conserve local endangered bat species properly due to lacking knowledge.

Solution

Create a species distribution model that can be used for better visualization of the distribution of bats in the Philippines that can be used for further research and conservation efforts.

Hypothesis

Bat populations are negatively impacted by factors related to climate change such as extreme weather.

Data

We shall be utilizing BatMapPH, a bat species occurence database published by the Eco/Con Lab Biodiversity Synthesis+ Centre. The data contains the taxonomic informationabout the species observed as well as coordinates. For the column decriptions, please refer to the GBIF documentation.

Scope

The data covers occurence data of bat species in the Philippines observed from January 1, 2000 - May 5, 2024.

Sampling

There are 1,739 occurences in the database gathered from compiling published electronic work concerning bats in the philippines. Namely, studies found on Google Scholar, Biodiversity Literature Repository and ResearchGate were collated.

Prepocessing

There are observations that only contains genus data, having missing species data. These will be discarded.

Let's talk about our data science methodology.

In order to find which environmental factors affect bat populations, we compared our occurrence data to WorldClim’s bioclimatic variables. We took their ssp585 CMIP6 downscaled future climate projections with a temporal scope of 2021-2040 and a resolution of 2.5 arc minutes and attempted to find a statistical relationship to the BatMapPh data.


We used linear regression as our primary statistical method to examine the relationship between environmental factors and bat occurrence. Our methodology began with basic data cleaning then training the regression model to estimate the linear relationship. Afterward, we assessed its performance using R².

Temperature and precipitation were selected as the main predictors (qualitative independent variables), while bat occurrences served as the response (qualitative dependent variable). Due to the independent and dependent variables being qualitative, we utilized a regression model to analyze the relationship between them. The dataset was split into training and testing sets to develop and validate the model. We trained the regression model to estimate the linear relationship and assessed its performance using metrics such as R², mean squared error (MSE), and mean absolute error (MAE). This approach provided a structured way to evaluate the combined impact of environmental factors and set a foundation for incorporating additional variables in future research.

Data Preprocessing

We first took the raster (GeoTiff) files containing the bioclimatic data and reformatted it to csv Pandas could read. Then, missing values were dropped (in particular the ocean was void of data) before performing a spatial join with our occurrence data.

Hypothesis Testing

We employed linear regression to evaluate and predict how changes in temperature influence bat occurrence. By modeling bat occurrence as a dependent variable and temperature as the independent variable, the regression analysis allowed us to quantify the relationship between these two factors.

Results

According to Festa et al, increased temperature can negatively affect bat populations. To test this, a linear regression was performed on bioclimatic variables to try and find a relationship between a certain variable – such as mean annual temperature – and bat occurrence. What was found was that simple regression models were weakly fit to one variable. Thus, a more complex model is needed.
